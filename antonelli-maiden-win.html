Kimi Antonelli Wins Maiden Formula 1 Race at Chinese Grand Prix

Mercedes youngster claims historic first victory as Hamilton secures first Ferrari podium in dramatic Shanghai race

By F1wow Team 5 min read

Kimi Antonelli has written his name into Formula 1 history books, claiming his maiden Grand Prix victory at the 2026 Chinese Grand Prix. The Mercedes youngster crossed the line 5.515 seconds ahead of teammate George Russell, while Lewis Hamilton secured his first podium for Ferrari.

🏁

Race Summary

The Shanghai International Circuit witnessed a historic moment as 20-year-old Kimi Antonelli became the first rookie since 2022 to win on his maiden Formula 1 victory. Starting from pole position, Antonelli controlled the race from the front, managing tire degradation perfectly in the tricky Chinese conditions.

The race was characterized by intense battles throughout the field, with the Mercedes duo displaying superior pace. George Russell finished second, completing a Mercedes 1-2 finish, while Lewis Hamilton's third place marked a significant milestone for Ferrari.

Key Moments

🟢 Lights Out

Antonelli made a perfect start from pole position, fending off an aggressive challenge from Russell into Turn 1. The Mercedes duo established a gap immediately.

🔄 Pit Stop Battles

Mercedes executed perfect pit stops under the first safety car period, allowing both cars to maintain track position. Ferrari's Hamilton jumped Leclerc through strategy.

🏆 Final Stint

Antonelli managed his fresher tires expertly, opening a gap to Russell while maintaining enough pace to secure the fastest lap bonus point.

🔴

Hamilton's Historic Ferrari Podium

Lewis Hamilton's third-place finish marks his first podium for Ferrari, a significant milestone in the seven-time world champion's career with the Italian team. After joining Ferrari in 2026, this result validates the team's progress under the new regulations.

"This is special," Hamilton said after the race. "The team has worked incredibly hard, and to be on the podium in our early days together shows we're heading in the right direction."

📊

Championship Standings

The result shakes up the early championship standings, with Antonelli closing the gap to teammate Russell. Mercedes now leads the constructors' championship by a significant margin.
