Verstappen Criticizes 2026 F1 Cars: 'It's a Joke'

Max Verstappen launches scathing attack on new 2026 regulations after Chinese GP testing, calling the future of Formula 1 'a joke'

By F1wow Team 4 min read

"It's a joke. I'd say the same thing if I won. If anyone actually finds this funny, then they don't understand what racing is all about." — Max Verstappen's scathing attack on the 2026 regulations has sent shockwaves through the Formula 1 paddock.

💥

What Sparked the Outburst?

Verstappen's frustration reportedly boiled over during the post-race debrief at the Shanghai International Circuit. While the exact incident that triggered his response remains unclear, sources close to the Red Bull camp suggest it relates to recent simulations and testing data regarding the 2026 car concepts.

The Dutchman's emphatic statement – 'I'd say the same thing if I won' – indicates this isn't sour grapes from a particular result, but rather a fundamental concern about the direction Formula 1 is heading.

⚙️

The 2026 Regulations: What's Changing?

The 2026 season represents the biggest technical revolution in modern F1 history. Key changes include:

  • Active Aerodynamics: Moveable front and rear wings that adjust automatically to reduce drag on straights and increase downforce in corners
  • New Power Units: 50% electric power (up from around 25% currently) with approximately 1000bhp combined output
  • No More MGU-H: The complex energy recovery from the turbocharger is being removed
  • Sustainable Fuels: 100% sustainable fuel drops the carbon footprint while maintaining performance
  • Reduced Drag: Cars designed to slipstream more easily for closer racing
🏎️

Why Verstappen Is Concerned

Verstappen has long been an advocate for purity in racing – the idea that the driver should be the primary differentiator, not the car's technological gimmicks. His 'they don't understand what racing is all about' comment appears directed at the increased automation and artificiality that active aerodynamics could introduce.

There are also concerns that the 2026 cars could be significantly slower despite the increased power, due to the aggressive drag reduction targets set by the FIA. Early simulations suggest lap times could increase by several seconds at certain circuits.

👥

Not Just Verstappen's Concern

Verstappen isn't alone in his skepticism. Several senior figures within the paddock have expressed private reservations about whether the 2026 rules will achieve their goals of closer racing and improved sustainability without compromising the essence of Formula 1.

Lewis Hamilton previously described the active aero concept as 'like playing a video game,' while Fernando Alonso has questioned whether the removal of the MGU-H might actually make the power units less road-relevant.

🔮

What Happens Next?

With the 2026 regulations now locked in and teams already deep into development, Verstappen's comments are unlikely to result in any changes. However, his voice carries significant weight in the F1 community, and his public criticism could put pressure on the FIA to ensure the sporting regulations complement the technical rules in a way that preserves authentic racing.

The Chinese Grand Prix was the first opportunity this season for drivers to experience early prototypes and simulations of what 2026 might look like. If Verstappen's reaction is anything to go by, the new era may face some fierce criticism from its biggest stars.
